## Design Context

### Users
Public readers and journalists encountering the PSN Merauke story as an explanatory, evidence-led web narrative. They need to quickly understand what is at stake, where it is happening, which actors and policies are involved, and how the spatial evidence connects to testimony and documented impacts.

### Brand Personality
Solemn, documentary, investigative. The interface should feel like a careful field dossier: restrained, credible, human-centred, and spatially precise, without becoming monotonous or visually flat.

### Aesthetic Direction
Indonesian-only for now. Use a reportorial scrollytelling map rather than a dashboard or campaign splash page. Favour warm paper tones, deep map contrast, archival image treatment, measured typography, and selective colour for evidence layers. Avoid generic dark glass panels, decorative gradients, and over-loud activist poster styling.

### Design Principles
- Lead with evidence: every visual state should clarify a place, policy, actor, or impact.
- Keep the human stakes present without sensationalising the story.
- Let the map and narrative take turns carrying attention; do not show every layer all the time.
- Make active chapter, map state, and source context obvious for readers who skim.
- Use variation in spacing, panel rhythm, image placement, and chapter metadata to avoid a repetitive card-scroll pattern.
